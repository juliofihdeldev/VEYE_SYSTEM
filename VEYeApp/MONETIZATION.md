# VEYe — Monetization Plan

---

## 1. Direct Revenue

### Freemium — Premium Subscription ($2–5/month)

Keep the core app free and charge for advanced features:

| Free | Premium |
|---|---|
| Alerts in your area | Alerts for multiple saved locations |
| Standard alert radius | Unlimited custom radius |
| Basic map view | Offline map access |
| Push notifications | SMS/WhatsApp alerts as backup |
| — | Priority verified reports (faster queue) |
| — | Travel safety briefings |

Target audience: **Haitian diaspora** watching over family still in Haiti.

---

### Family Safety Plan ($5–10/month)

A paid plan where one person (e.g. in diaspora) monitors multiple family members' locations and receives alerts on their behalf — like "Life360 for Haiti."

- Monitor up to 5 family members
- Receive alerts on their behalf even if they don't have the app
- Alert history and timeline per family member

---

### B2B / Institutional Licenses ($50–200/month per org)

Sell access to organizations operating in Haiti:

- NGOs and humanitarian organizations (MSF, UN, Red Cross)
- Embassies (US, France, Canada) for staff safety
- Private security companies managing field teams
- Journalists and media organizations covering Haiti

Deliverables: dashboard access (existing VEYeDashboard), bulk alerts, data export, and API access.

---

### Verified Reporter Badge ($10–20/year)

Community reporters pay a small yearly fee for:

- A "Verified" badge on their reports
- Reports bypass the moderation queue (faster publication)
- Builds trust and credibility in the community

---

## 2. Features That Increase Value

### Travel Safety Score

Before entering an area, show a safety score (1–10) based on recent alert density.

- Free users: city-level score
- Premium users: neighborhood-level score
- B2B: available as a paid API for travel apps and tour operators

---

### Emergency Contact Alerts

When a user enters a detected danger zone, automatically notify their emergency contacts via SMS or WhatsApp.

- High-value feature for families and solo travelers
- Premium tier feature

---

### Safe Route Suggestions

Display routes on the map that avoid current active danger zones — similar to Waze but for personal safety.

- Useful for daily commuters and drivers in Port-au-Prince
- Premium tier feature

---

### Weekly Safety Digest (Email / Push)

Premium subscribers receive a weekly summary of alerts in their saved areas.

- Useful for diaspora who check less frequently
- Summary includes: alert count by type, most active zones, resolved cases

---

### Business Safe Zone Listings (Monthly fee per listing)

Allow businesses (hotels, restaurants, schools, clinics) to pay to add a "Safe Zone" marker on the map with their verified address and safety status.

- Visible to all users on the map
- Small monthly fee per business listing
- Builds a network of trusted safe locations

---

## 3. Acquisition / Exit Strategy

### Sell to a Larger Platform

VEYe has real, verified data and a niche audience that is hard to build from scratch. Potential acquirers:

- **Security intelligence platforms** — Crisis24, OSAC, Dataminr
- **Travel insurance companies** — need real-time risk data for policy pricing
- **Telecom companies in Haiti** — Digicel, Natcom could bundle it as a value-added service
- **International news organizations** — Reuters, AP need verified incident data for coverage

---

### Data Licensing (Anonymized API)

The anonymized, verified alert data collected is valuable to:

- Academic researchers studying crime, conflict, and urban safety
- International risk assessment and consulting firms
- Government agencies and NGOs planning operations in Haiti

Offer a paid API or data subscription with anonymized, aggregated exports.

---

## 4. Quick Wins (Low Effort, High Impact)

These can be implemented quickly with high return:

1. **Diaspora subscription** — monitored saved locations for a monthly fee. The diaspora community is the most motivated paying audience.

2. **NGO / Embassy B2B dashboard** — the VEYeDashboard already exists. Add a billing layer and outreach to humanitarian organizations operating in Haiti.

3. **Donate / Support button** — many safety apps for underserved communities do well with voluntary donations from the diaspora. Integrate with Stripe or Buy Me a Coffee / Patreon.
